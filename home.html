Waste Water

Wastewater is any water that has been used and is no longer suitable for its intended purpose. It typically contains a variety of contaminants, including organic matter, chemicals, and microorganisms, which can be harmful if not properly treated. Wastewater is generated from various sources, including residential, industrial, and commercial activities.

Wastewater and Its Treatment

Learn about the parameters affecting wastewater treatment and their significance.The parameters include:

pH

pH is a measure of the relative amount of free hydrogen and hydroxyl ions in a substance.

Turbidity

The ability of water to conduct electricity, indicating the presence of dissolved ions and salts.

Hardness

The hardness of water is mainly contributed by calcium and magnesium ions.

Sulfates

Sulfate is one of the major dissolved components of rain.

Conductivity

The ability of water to conduct electricity, indicating the presence of dissolved ions and salts.

Organic Carbon

A measure of organic matter in water, indicating the amount of carbon-based compounds.

HaloMethanes

Particles suspended in water scatter light, indicating the presence of impurities.

Chloramines

Chemical compounds formed by mixing chlorine with ammonia.

Solids

Particles suspended or dissolved in water, including organic and inorganic matter.
